## Executive Summary

The HR Department achieved a **-8.2% budget underspend** in Q1 2026, with actual expenditure of AED 2,295,000 against an approved budget of AED 2,500,000, resulting in a variance of -AED 205,000.

This variance is classified as **MINOR** under the Financial Management Act 2024.

## Detailed Justification

### 1. Recruitment (-AED 250,000) — UNDERSPEND

**Primary Cause:** Delayed hiring due to lengthy government approval processes.

**Details:**
- 12 approved positions remained unfilled throughout Q1
- 6 positions: awaiting security clearance (8-12 week process)
- 4 positions: budget approval delays from Ministry of Finance
- 2 positions: failed to find qualified candidates meeting government pay scales

**Impact:**
- Saved recruitment agency fees: -AED 150,000
- Saved onboarding costs: -AED 60,000
- Saved relocation packages: -AED 40,000

**Forecast:** Expect to fill 8 positions in Q2, 4 positions in Q3. Budget will normalize.

---

### 2. Training & Development (+AED 50,000) — MINOR OVERSPEND

**Primary Cause:** Leadership development program for digital transformation.

**Details:**
- Executive coaching for 15 senior managers: +AED 40,000
- Emergency cybersecurity awareness training (post-incident): +AED 10,000

**Justification:**
Following the IT security incident in February, mandatory cybersecurity awareness training was required for all staff. This was an unplanned but necessary expenditure to meet compliance requirements.

---

### 3. Employee Benefits (-AED 5,000) — ACCEPTABLE

**Minor underspend:** Wellness program participation lower than projected (90% vs 95% target).

---

## Remediation Plan

### Actions for Q2-Q4 2026
- Accelerate recruitment for 8 critical positions
- Budget surplus will be absorbed by Q2/Q3 hiring wave
- Request budget reallocation to IT department to support their Q1 critical variance
