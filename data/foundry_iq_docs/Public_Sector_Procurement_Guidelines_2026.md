# Public Sector Procurement Guidelines 2026

**Issued by:** Central Government Finance Authority  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Applies to:** All government entities and authorities  
**Compliance Status:** Mandatory

---

## 1. INTRODUCTION

These guidelines establish the framework for procurement and contract management across all public sector entities, including digital government authorities. Compliance is mandatory under the Public Finance Management Act.

---

## 2. PROCUREMENT THRESHOLDS

### 2.1 Competitive Bidding Requirements

| Procurement Value | Minimum Quotes | Approval Level | Timeline |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------|----------|
| < AED 50,000 | 1 (Direct award) | Department Head | 5 days |
| AED 50,000 - 200,000 | 3 written quotes | Department Head | 15 days |
| AED 200,000 - 1,000,000 | 5 written quotes | CFO | 30 days |
| AED 1,000,000 - 5,000,000 | Formal tender | Procurement Committee | 60 days |
| > AED 5,000,000 | Open tender | Board + Audit Committee | 90 days |

### 2.2 Emergency Procurement
In documented emergencies, thresholds may be waived with:
- Written justification from department head
- Post-approval ratification within 30 days
- Full audit trail maintained

Emergency procurement is limited to:
- Critical system failures
- Security breaches
- Natural disasters
- Urgent regulatory compliance

## 3. IT & TECHNOLOGY PROCUREMENT

### 3.1 Pre-Approved Frameworks
Government entities must use pre-approved frameworks for:
- **Cloud services:** Government Cloud Framework (Azure, AWS GovCloud)
- **Software licensing:** Enterprise agreement schemes
- **Hardware:** Government Hardware Panel (HP, Dell, Lenovo)
- **Professional services:** Approved vendor panel

### 3.2 IT Security Requirements
All IT procurements must include:
- Vendor security assessment
- Data residency compliance (in-country for sensitive data)
- Encryption standards (minimum AES-256)
- Incident response SLAs
- Right to audit clauses

### 3.3 Software Licensing Compliance
1. Open-source software preferred where suitable
2. Vendor lock-in risk assessment mandatory
3. Exit clauses required for SaaS >3-year commitments
4. License true-up audits annually

## 4. CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Contract Terms
Standard contract terms for IT services:
- **Payment terms:** Net 30 days
- **Warranty period:** Minimum 12 months
- **SLA penalties:** Minimum 5% of monthly fee per breach
- **Termination for convenience:** 90 days notice
- **Force majeure:** Standard government clauses

### 4.2 Contract Amendments
Contract value increases require:
- < 10% increase: Department head approval
- 10-25% increase: CFO approval + documented justification
- > 25% increase: Re-tendering mandatory (except for sole-source)

---

## 5. VARIANCE IMPACT ON PROCUREMENT

### 5.1 Budget Variance and Procurement Authority
When a department experiences budget variance:

**MINOR Variance (5-10%)**:
- ✅ Existing contracts can proceed
- ⚠️ New procurements require additional justification
- ⚠️ Non-essential purchases should be deferred

**SIGNIFICANT Variance (10-25%)**:
- ⚠️ CFO pre-approval for all new procurements >AED 50,000
- ❌ Non-critical procurements should be paused
- ✅ Critical operational needs can proceed with justification

**CRITICAL Variance (>25%)**:
- ❌ Procurement freeze on all non-essential purchases
- ✅ Only emergency/critical procurements allowed
- ✅ Board approval required for any new contracts >AED 100,000

### 5.2 Underspend and Procurement Planning
Departments with >15% underspend must:
- Provide utilization report to CFO
- Accelerate planned procurements if viable
- Risk losing unspent budget in future allocations

---

## 6. VENDOR MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Vendor Performance Monitoring
All vendors must be rated quarterly on:
- Delivery timeliness
- Quality of deliverables
- Responsiveness
- Cost management
- Security compliance

Poor-performing vendors may be removed from approved panels.

### 6.2 Vendor Diversity
Government entities should aim for:
- 30% spend with small-medium enterprises (SMEs)
- 20% spend with local vendors
- Consideration for social enterprises

---

## 7. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

### 7.1 Procurement Audit Requirements
All procurements >AED 1,000,000 subject to:
- Internal audit review
- Compliance certification
- Board Audit Committee reporting

### 7.2 Non-Compliance Penalties
- Unauthorized procurement: Reprimand + budget suspension
- Fraud or corruption: Termination + legal action
- Repeated non-compliance: Procurement authority revocation

---

## 8. SUSTAINABILITY AND VALUE FOR MONEY

### 8.1 Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
Procurement decisions must consider:
- Initial purchase cost
- Maintenance and support costs (3-5 year projection)
- Training and change management costs
- Decommissioning/exit costs
- Environmental impact

### 8.2 Value for Money Assessment
Lowest price is not the sole criterion. Evaluation includes:
- Technical capability (40%)
- Price competitiveness (30%)
- Vendor track record (15%)
- Sustainability (10%)
- Innovation (5%)

---

## 9. SPECIAL PROVISIONS FOR DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION

### 9.1 Agile Procurement for IT Projects
Recognizing the iterative nature of IT projects:
- Multi-year framework agreements permitted
- Phased delivery with milestone-based payments
- Flexibility for scope adjustments (within ±15%)
- Quarterly value reviews

### 9.2 Cloud Services Procurement
Cloud services have modified rules:
- Pay-as-you-go contracts allowed (no fixed commitment)
- Monthly cost reviews mandatory
- Auto-scaling within approved budget limits
- Reserved instance commitments require business case

---

## 10. 2026 PRIORITIES

### 10.1 Digital Procurement Platform
All government entities must transition to the Central Procurement Portal by Q3 2026:
- E-tendering for all procurements >AED 200,000
- Vendor self-service portal
- Real-time spend visibility
- Automated compliance checks

### 10.2 Sustainability Goals
2026 targets for government procurement:
- 50% reduction in paper-based processes
- 25% of IT hardware from circular economy sources
- 100% of data centers to use renewable energy
