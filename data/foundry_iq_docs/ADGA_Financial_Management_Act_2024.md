# Apex Digital Government Authority
## Financial Management Act 2024

**Act No. 12 of 2024**  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** December 15, 2023

---

## PART I: PRELIMINARY

### Section 1: Short Title and Commencement
This Act may be cited as the Apex Digital Government Authority Financial Management Act 2024 and shall come into operation on January 1, 2024.

### Section 2: Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
- **"Approved budget"** means the annual expenditure plan approved by the Board of Directors
- **"Budget variance"** means any deviation exceeding 5% from the approved budget allocation
- **"Critical variance"** means any overspend exceeding 25% of the approved budget
- **"CFO"** means the Chief Financial Officer of the Authority
- **"Board"** means the Board of Directors of the Authority

---

## PART II: BUDGET APPROVAL AND MANAGEMENT

### Section 3: Annual Budget Approval Process
1. The CFO shall prepare an annual budget for the forthcoming fiscal year by October 31
2. The budget shall be reviewed by the Finance Committee within 15 business days
3. Final approval shall be granted by the Board of Directors by December 15
4. Approved budgets shall be allocated by department and quarter

### Section 4: Quarterly Budget Reviews
1. Department heads must submit quarterly expenditure reports within 10 business days of quarter end
2. Variance reports must include:
   - Actual expenditure vs approved budget
   - Explanation for variances exceeding 5%
   - Corrective actions for overspends
   - Forecast for remaining quarters

---

## PART III: VARIANCE THRESHOLDS AND ESCALATION

### Section 5: Variance Classification
Budget variances shall be classified as follows:

| Variance % | Classification | Required Action |
|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| ≤ 5% | ACCEPTABLE | No action required |
| 5-10% | MINOR | Department head review |
| 10-25% | SIGNIFICANT | CFO approval required |
| > 25% | CRITICAL | Board notification mandatory |
| < -15% (underspend) | REVIEW | Investigation of underutilization |

### Section 6: Escalation Procedures for Overspend
1. **MINOR (5-10%)**:
   - Department head must document justification
   - No formal approval required
   - CFO notification recommended

2. **SIGNIFICANT (10-25%)**:
   - Written request to CFO required within 5 business days
   - CFO must approve or reject within 10 business days
   - If approved, budget reallocation plan must be submitted
   - Finance Committee notification required

3. **CRITICAL (>25%)**:
   - Immediate Board notification required
   - Spending freeze on affected cost center
   - Emergency Board meeting within 15 business days
   - Full audit of expenditure required
   - Department head must present remediation plan

### Section 7: Underspend Investigation
1. Underspend exceeding 15% shall trigger a utilization review
2. Department heads must explain reasons for underutilization
3. Unspent funds may be reallocated to other priorities
4. Persistent underspend may result in future budget reductions

---

## PART IV: PROCUREMENT AND SPENDING AUTHORITIES

### Section 8: Spending Limits by Role
1. **Department Heads**: Up to AED 100,000 per transaction
2. **Deputy Heads**: Up to AED 50,000 per transaction
3. **CFO**: Up to AED 500,000 per transaction
4. **Director General**: Up to AED 2,000,000 per transaction
5. **Board Approval Required**: Above AED 2,000,000

### Section 9: Emergency Spending Provisions
1. In cases of operational emergency, the Director General may authorize spending up to AED 5,000,000
2. Emergency spending must be ratified by the Board within 30 days
3. Definition of "emergency" includes:
   - Critical system failures
   - Security breaches
   - Natural disasters affecting operations
   - Urgent regulatory compliance requirements

---

## PART V: FINANCIAL REPORTING

### Section 10: Mandatory Financial Reports
1. **Monthly**: Cash flow statements to CFO
2. **Quarterly**: Variance reports to Finance Committee
3. **Annually**: Audited financial statements to Board

### Section 11: Variance Report Requirements
All quarterly variance reports must include:
1. Detailed breakdown by cost center and category
2. Comparison with prior year same quarter
3. Analysis of variance drivers (external vs internal factors)
4. Risk assessment for remainder of fiscal year
5. Corrective action plans with timelines
6. Sign-off by department head and deputy head

---

## PART VI: COMPLIANCE AND PENALTIES

### Section 12: Non-Compliance Penalties
1. Failure to report variances within prescribed timeframes: Written warning
2. Unapproved spending exceeding authority: Disciplinary review
3. Fraudulent reporting: Termination and legal action
4. Repeated non-compliance: Budget authority suspension

### Section 13: Audit and Oversight
1. The Internal Audit team shall conduct quarterly compliance reviews
2. External auditors shall conduct annual financial audits
3. Audit findings must be addressed within 90 days
4. The Board Audit Committee oversees compliance

---

## PART VII: MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 14: Budget Amendment Process
1. Mid-year budget amendments may be requested in writing to the CFO
2. Amendments require Finance Committee approval
3. Amendments exceeding 10% of total budget require Board approval

### Section 15: Review of this Act
This Act shall be reviewed every three years, with the next review due January 1, 2027.
